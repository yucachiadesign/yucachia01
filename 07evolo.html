SCENARIO:

Since mankind started to transform the external world, there have been three key elements influencing urban development form-- - ideology, levels of material production and science and technology, and social and political culture. The interaction of them effected series of development policies, Athens Charter (1933), The Charter of Machu Picchu (1977) and Quito Declaration (2016) etc. In the summer of 1933, The fourth CIAM conferenc marked a group of architects headed by Le Corbusier who began to theorise their vision of "the functional city". The outcome of this conference was finalised in Athens Charter published in 1943. In the long post-war years Athens Charter was widely circulated as a symbol and criterion of urban construction and directly shaped today's stereotyped cityscape, high-rise buildings, squares, green areas and avenues. Since that day, mankind has begun to chase high and higher building. Facing severe climate change, ascendant big data, and urban informality，Quito Declaration decided to completely negate Corbusier, which intended to reconstruct urban space through improved design strategy and empower all of urban residents to create their own new life. The reuse of second-hand space shortens the urban space and finally form a world without skies.
